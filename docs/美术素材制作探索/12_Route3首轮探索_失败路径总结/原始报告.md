### Result

- best slot: `v1_atlas_crisp_no_stitch`.
- failed slots: no technical failure; `v0` is softer and `v2` is slightly cleaner/sharper but does not add enough volume.
- seam assessment: no-stitch ink is useful and should stay in candidate testing. It keeps wall-wall seams from becoming black cracks.
- style assessment: edge settings help less than expected because the selected material source is still too flat on top. The mesh/edge preset cannot recreate the r2 left 3D target without better rim/material input.
- Blender controllability: Blender can control stitch ink and bevel character, but needs a better top-rim/material source or a shader/mesh rim layer to reach the target.

### Decision

Keep `v1_atlas_crisp_no_stitch` as the current best Blender preset for this material source. Do not promote to production defaults yet.

### Next Adjustment

Test continuous atlas plus a dedicated top grass lip / moss rim layer, or a Blender procedural rim mesh, before further tiny bevel/ink tuning.

## Overall Decision

- Best structural route: Group B / Group D continuous wall strip atlas.
- Best current visual slot: `group_d v1_atlas_crisp_no_stitch`.
- Paper-net route: technically viable but aesthetically weak; seam cleanup alone cannot make it match the 3D target.
- Continuous wall strip: improves side-wall continuity and reduces wall-wall black seams.
- Biggest remaining gap: top rim and material richness. The AI 3D target has a thick grass lip, stronger top-wall transition, and richer edge mass. The right atlas material source does not yet encode that well, and Blender currently has no separate rim mesh/shader layer to recreate it.

## Shadow / Lighting Conclusion

The r2 prompt kept the atlas as albedo/material input (`no cast shadow`, `no drop shadow`, `no baked lighting`). The baked scene did not show obvious double-shadow issues on the new candidates. The strategy should remain:

- UV/atlas = albedo + material detail.
- Blender = lighting, shadow, bevel, silhouette, and ink edges.
