# Blender-Owned Tile Seam Prototype

## Result

Best preview is round 10: same-height tiles now use the Godot tile-pipeline projection and complete-sprite painter order, then receive a narrow dark contact seam plus a restrained warm highlight on the lit side. It avoids the heavy black/double outline from whole-map image generation and keeps the six tile surfaces independent.

## Validation

- Shared edges generated once: `42` deterministic edges.
- Map cells: `19` cells using all six raw tile types.
- Projection matches `InkMonRender2DIsoProjection.ground_basis()` and draw order matches `InkMonTilePipelineScene`: sort complete tile sprites by projected center `y`, then `x`, before compositing.
- Best draw mode: `full_sprite_painter`.
- White-pixel ratio in best preview: `0.000001`, so no large white edge/background artifact is present.
- `map_no_seam_baseline.png` shows the raw contact gaps; `map_blender_seam_preview.png` covers them with owned seam geometry.
- The raw tile contact sheet confirms each tile remains an independent single-tile surface.

## Failed / Rejected Directions

- Round 1 was too close to a black outline.
- Round 2 still had too much continuous highlight.
- Round 3 got clean but too flat.
- Round 4 restored volume but made seam intersections too dark.
- Round 5 fixed intersections but left a few exterior white wall slivers.
- Round 6 reduced the slivers by insetting wall masks but did not eliminate all bottom-edge white pixels.
- Round 7 removed exterior white slivers but still let raw AI top-edge ink dominate shared seams.
- Round 8 reduced raw edge ink but exposed same-height YSort errors because foreground top faces had been inset too far and the candidate layout did not follow the project painter reference.
- Round 9 used the right projected order but still invented a wall/top split that does not match `tile_pipeline_scene.gd`'s complete `Sprite2D` child ordering.
- Whole-map image generation was not revisited.

## Why Stop Here

Round 10 is the first pass that follows the project's existing complete-sprite YSort/painter contract and still reads as a narrow crevice at normal zoom. More seam tuning now should happen against direct user visual judgment instead of changing the layout model again.
